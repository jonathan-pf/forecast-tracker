## Key Takeaways

**Biggest mover: UK CPI Inflation (−25pp, from 33% to 8%)**
The Bank of England's March 2026 Monetary Policy Summary delivered a major upward revision to the inflation outlook, projecting CPI at 3–3.5% throughout Q2–Q3 2026 due to elevated energy prices linked to Middle East conflict. This makes a sub-2% CPI reading before January 2027 extremely unlikely. Current CPI is running at 3%.

**Notable upward revision: Spider-Man: Brand New Day (+2pp, now 88%)**
The film's trailer became the first in cinema history to cross 1 billion views. Box office tracking analysts now project $2.54B worldwide, making the $1.5B threshold appear comfortably within reach for the July 31 release.

**Moderate downward revisions:**
- **OpenAI + Anthropic ARR (−8pp, now 74%):** New data reveals a combined run-rate of ~$44B (OpenAI ~$25B + Anthropic ~$19B) in March 2026. Reaching $70B combined by Q4 requires approximately 60% growth over 9 months — achievable given Anthropic's explosive trajectory, but less certain than previously assessed.
- **UK GDP 2030s (−5pp, now 44%):** OBR's March 2026 forecast reduced 2026 growth to just 1.1%, weakening the case for the 2030s to average above 1.5%.
- **Labour largest party (−3pp, now 11%):** Reform UK holds a consistent 7-point polling lead with MRP modelling suggesting Labour could finish third in seats at the next election. The breadth of Labour's electoral squeeze (Reform on the right, Greens on the left) is historically unusual.
